# Variant 08 — Botanical Line-Art Frame

**Direction:** Frame the headline with fine terracotta line-art botanical illustrations — like a Victorian herbarium plate. The plants are the frame; the type is centered within.

**Signature move:**
- Two SVG botanical specimens flanking the headline: a Boston fern frond on the left, an olive branch on the right (or any two distinct biophilic plants — research real botanical line drawings for accuracy)
- Both drawn in fine terracotta `#B46A2C` strokes, ~1px, with hand-drawn variation
- Each plant labeled with tiny Latin name in italic Garamond at 0.7rem (e.g., `Nephrolepis exaltata` and `Olea europaea`)
- Center: the headline in Garamond, with `Oikos.` in Garamond Roman top, `for a life that` smaller mid, `sustains you.` italic terracotta bottom
- Below the headline: a thin double-rule + the about paragraph centered, max-width 50ch
- Subtle on-load animation: the SVG paths draw in via `stroke-dasharray` over 1.6s

**Inspiration tone:** Victorian herbarium plates, Linnaeus's Species Plantarum, Audubon's Birds of America

**Constraints:**
- The botanical SVGs MUST be hand-illustrated quality — not generic clipart. Use bezier curves, vary stroke widths slightly per leaf, mimic real pen-and-ink fern/olive illustrations. Spend real care here.
- Latin names in Garamond italic, lowercase except first letter of genus
- Background cream only, no photos
- Layout symmetric (mirrored plants flanking centered type)
