# Variant 05 — Greek Antiquity Watermark

**Direction:** Honor the etymology. The Greek word `οἶκος` is rendered as a massive ghost watermark behind the readable English headline — like layered serif on serif, ancient on contemporary. Visual depth via type alone.

**Signature move:**
- Background layer: `οἶκος` in Garamond italic at 30vw font-size, color cream-deep `#F3E6C9` (just barely visible against `#FFF8EB`), positioned center, slightly tilted (~-2deg) or with slight letter-spacing animation that subtly pulses
- Foreground layer: the English headline in normal hierarchy, sitting ON TOP of the Greek watermark
- The contrast: the Greek is HUGE but barely visible (memory of origin), the English is PRESENT (the brand today)
- Optional: a tiny Greek phrase below the headline like `– οἶκος, n. household, ecosystem –` in tiny Garamond italic terracotta

**Inspiration tone:** Cy Twombly's painted text, Jenny Holzer's projections, archaeological museum signage

**Constraints:**
- The Greek MUST be authentic Unicode (`οἶκος` with proper diacritics) — load Garamond from Google Fonts with the `greek` subset
- No photographs
- The layering must work: foreground readable, background ghosting through
- Subtle animation on the Greek (pulse or breathe) is OK; aggressive motion is slop
